All About Me

Hi there! My name is Ayah, I am 20 years old and currently enrolled in the Interactive Media Design program at Algonquin College. This is my 1st year in college and I'm already having a great time so far! I used to be a student at Carleton University and studied Criminology/Law for a semester and a half before realizing I had no real passion for legal work and wanted to do something more creative instead. I don't have a lot of prior experience in most of my classes like photography, digital graphics, and web design, however, I've always wanted to learn so I enjoy the challenge and feel proud of myself whenever I complete my work well. I'm also surprised at how easy it is to meet new people and make new friends, I am not really a social person so I'm glad I have people I can hang out with on campus. Another thing I never expected was being chosen as the class rep for this semester, I just volunteered for the role last minute without thinking, but I'm glad I got chosen because I already see some things within our program that could be changed for the better and I'd love to be able to make a difference and make sure my group and I have a great 1st semester!

"I am becoming the woman I've envisioned to be in the future by doing the things I need to do today" - Ayah Ebada

Outside of my student life, I work at BWCH after class and help students from grades 1-6 with their homework. It's the best job I've gotten so far, and I love connecting and joking around with the kids. I think because I am a little bit younger than the average teacher, the students don't have a hard time opening up to me because the superiority gap is not that big, a lot of the younger girls see me as an older sister which is super cute; I try my best to be a good influence for them. It's funny because I am an older sister outside of work too. I think I'm good at my job because I've had a lot of practice taking care of little kids since I am the 2nd mom for my younger siblings. I have a younger brother and sister that are both in high school and a 4 year old baby brother. My house is always loud, my leftovers are always eaten, and my baby brother always runs into my room and goes through my things whenever I'm doing homework, but I wouldn't have it any other way. I am grateful to not only be able to study what I want to take/have a passion for, but also have my family and close friends support me along the way. I'm very proud of what I've been doing so far so I can't wait to see the results of my hard work.
